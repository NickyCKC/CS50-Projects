Anglican High School

At the age of 9, I started watching basketball for the first time.

I was amazed by the Heats VS Mavericks NBA match

Ever since then, I have been playing basketball outside on my free time for fun

With no offical training before, I managed to get accepted to a school

with a long and rich history in basketball.

Little did I know how intense and demanding their trainings were as I struggled as a beginner.

After 1 year of training, I managed to clinch the title of Vice-captain, giving me the motivation to push my limits.

My role on the team was the power forward as a had I larger build compared to my teammates.

My skills slowly grew as I gritted teeth through the tough trainings.

By the time I graduated, I walked away with 3 First Runner-up medals for the 4 annual seasons I took part in.

Kai Xuan

During my final year in Anglican High, I was persuaded to join Kai Xuan,

an external basketball club with strong roots to Christianity.

It was during their trainings that my skill cap increased as I had more opportunities

to shoot long range shots and dribble the ball more.

I only stayed in the club for 1 year and lost

in the only competition I took part in.

Raffles Institution

When I joined this school at the age of 17,

I knew the team was going to struggle during competitions

as there were no tall people to play centers or power forwards.

I was the only one playing the center position, which wasn't my strongest position on court.

The first season ended abruptly as we lost two crucial matches by a few points only.

It was a tough loss to take as I knew we had the potential to become the champs that year.

The following year, I got elected as the Vice-captian once again, but my teammates that I had

were mostly the ones who weren't selected for the first year's team so they were lacking in a few areas

I quickly enforced discipline and taught the weaker ones how to become better.

Within a few months time, the whole team had reliable players, ready to step-up when needed.

Although we didn't get to play a season in 2020 due to COVID-19,

we still managed to play friendly matches with local schools before the pandemic.

We destroyed every team who played agaisnt us,

and I was so proud to see how far my team has grown in a few short months.

If we played in season that year, I am confident we would make it to the Finals!

I was also mentioned on a local sports article, although the shot was quite unglamorous:)
